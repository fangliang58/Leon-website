Research

Ongoing Work & Projects

I came to academic research through practice — as a founder, investor, and incubator manager — long before I knew I wanted a PhD.

Origin

My early work was practitioner research — due diligence reports and market analyses built to support investment decisions at ZhenFund and DT Capital. Not academic in form, but formative: I learned to find signal fast and synthesize across incomplete information.

Now

I'm working on two formal projects: empirical labor economics with Prof. Hwang at Kellogg (Stata, spatial analysis), and field research on venture formation with Prof. Lane at Harvard's LISH lab (survey design, founder interviews, AI/LLM pipeline).

Methods

I work across qualitative and quantitative traditions. Particularly comfortable with survey design and administration, semi-structured interviews, and building data pipelines that bridge unstructured inputs with analysis-ready outputs.
